Exhibition: The Listening Eye

In 2024, I worked on the production of The Listening Eye exhibition for Pro Artibus and Chappe. The exhibition was centered around eight themes, and featured over ninety works. I contributed by designing custom symbols, wall signage, and an exhibition booklet. The title is a reference to a work by constructivist Juhana Blomstedt, that served as the exhibition's thematical point. The exhibition was the first collaboration between the two institutions.

→ Exhibition design, icons, symbols, booklet, print

Research: Burana Card

The Burana Card is the result of a three-month Design Research module at FHNW HGK in 2020, under the mentorship of Dr. Paloma López Grüninger and Dr. Karel van der Waarde. For the project, I focused on simplifying Burana's instruction leaflets, as they are often too long and unclear. For the research, I decided to focus on young parents and caregivers who needed clearer dosage guidance for children. For the project I created concepts, conducted Zoom interviews and gathered design feedback from peers. The final design is a compact, color-coded, credit card-sized format with dosage visuals, a medical emergency number, and a QR code for access to the full leaflet.

→ Design research, infographics, concept development

Poster: Rück Tritt Käse

The theme of the poster relates to my past experience working in a high-risk environment and my fascination with German language while studying in Switzerland. One notable detail of the poster that is not easily demonstratable via screen, is the effect of the self-mixed white ink, that is extremely hi-viz as it consists of a silver pigment mixed with phosphorescent powder. Poster screenprinted and produced at FHNW | HGK Basel, under the mentorship of Jiri Oplatek, in 2021.

→ Poster design, typography, print, art direction, concept
